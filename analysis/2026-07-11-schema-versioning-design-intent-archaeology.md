## Verdict

**Corroborated, with one qualification.** The mechanism accreted piecemeal across roughly ten weeks (2026-05-09 → 2026-07-11). At no point in claude-history does a single planning conversation lay out all six version-shaped concepts and how they reconcile before any of them were built. Each session solved the next immediate problem in front of it. The qualification: the *earliest* slice (schema `$id`/`version` + the migration-registry primitives) was NOT ad hoc — it was step 4 of an explicit, pre-written, multi-step plan (the "pi-context rebuild arc"). But that plan's own stated scope was narrow (closing FGAP-006 + surfacing FGAP-016) and never claimed to be the full versioning contract. Everything built on top of it — config's own migrate-on-load, the block-wide `schema_version` envelope, migrations.json's own envelope version, and the `installed_schemas`/`installed_blocks`/install-baseline reconciliation — arrived later as separate, independently-discovered gaps that were only retroactively bound together into one feature once enough of them had piled up to look like a pattern.

## Bottom line for the user's original question

The extension code's version-shaped concepts are real and load-bearing (schema `version`, block `schema_version`, config's `schema_version`, migrations.json's own envelope, per-schema `fromVersion`/`toVersion`, and the install-baseline `installed_schemas`/`installed_blocks`/`installed_from`). claude-history shows they were built in that order, over ten weeks, by five-plus independent sessions each closing the gap in front of it — not from one design that was executed piece by piece. The one exception is the innermost primitive (schema `version` + migration registry itself), which WAS pre-planned as step 4 of a named multi-step arc; everything reconciling it with config/migrations.json/install-time behavior came later, unplanned, gap-by-gap. Your suspicion is correct for the mechanism as a whole; it is only partly correct for its oldest component.
