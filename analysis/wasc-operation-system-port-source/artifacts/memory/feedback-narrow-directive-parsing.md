When a directive concerns a specific artifact (a document, a section, a particular field), it concerns *only* that artifact. Do not extrapolate to adjacent files. Do not "also clean up" related places. Do not add editorial paragraphs to retroactively explain or justify the change. Authorization for X is not authorization for adjacent-to-X.

**Why:** On 2026-05-15, a directive to "remove Yungu and WeChat as not being part of scope" — which concerned the architect-review's framework recommendations — was misread as a global content directive. I deleted school plan content from `plan-data.js` (two Yungu proposals plus a glossary entry) that the directive never addressed, then added a framework-vs-content "critical distinction" paragraph to `DRAFT-VISION.md` to retroactively explain the cleanup. David flagged the sequence as dangerous, chaos-causing, and "psychotic" — the cascade of unauthorized actions far exceeded the narrow directive. The framework-vs-content distinction was confusion I generated by overreaching, not a clarification anyone needed.

**How to apply:** Every directive, especially ones that could plausibly extend to adjacent artifacts. Default to the narrowest accurate reading. Identify the specific artifact(s) and section(s) named. Touch only those. If a directive could plausibly cascade, stop and report rather than expand scope unilaterally. Editorial commentary about changes ("Critical distinction:...", "This is because...") belongs in the conversation reply, not inside the artifact. When the directive says "remove X from Y," confirm Y is the only scope before touching Y-adjacent artifacts. Related: [[mandate-001-no-unauthorized-action]].
