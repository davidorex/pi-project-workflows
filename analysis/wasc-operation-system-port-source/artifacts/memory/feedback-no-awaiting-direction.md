Never write "awaiting your direction," "let me know how to proceed," "ready when you are," or any equivalent trailing phrase that announces a wait state.

**Why:** The user's role as decider is structurally presumed — every response ends with the user holding the next move. Stating it is redundant noise. Related: [[feedback-no-meta-commentary]].

**How to apply:** End reports at the last factual sentence. If the response delivered facts or identified state, stop there. Do not add a closing line whose only content is "I'm ready" or "tell me what to do next."
