# Policy revision (6-step consultative)

## Step 1 — Senior Leadership Team convenes the Principal, Vice Principal for Academics, Vice Principal for Bilingual Programme, Heads of Early Years, Primary, and Secondary

**Description**

Senior Leadership Team convenes the Principal, Vice Principal for Academics, Vice Principal for Bilingual Programme, Heads of Early Years, Primary, and Secondary, the Heads of Chinese and English Departments, the Head of Co-Curricular Programme, the Head of Student Affairs, and the Head of Admissions and Communications to present options for the SLO descriptor-authoring work and the downstream SLO-tagging architecture. The meeting works from the existing Confident descriptor as the depth benchmark, names the six SLOs requiring new descriptors (Bilingual Communicators, Compassionate, Engaged, Innovative, Reflective, Responsible), tables three authoring-team configurations (cross-divisional by SLO, by-division then cross-validated, central drafting then divisional consultation), three SLO-tagging architectures for the unit-planning template and gradebook (per-unit one-or-two SLO field, per-assessment SLO field, both), and a calendar that lands authored descriptors by 2025-11-28, the Term 2 unit-planning rollover, the Term reporting cycles, and the self-study evidence binder by 2026-07-24. The meeting selects one authoring-team configuration and one tagging architecture and records the selection in the SLT minutes alongside the named owners for each of the seven descriptor-authoring teams and the named owners for the unit-template, gradebook, parent-report-template, and PLC-minutes-template revisions.

**Assessment**

SLT minutes record the selected authoring-team configuration, the selected SLO-tagging architecture, the named lead and members for each of the seven descriptor-authoring teams covering all seven SLOs, and the named owners for the unit-planning template revision, the gradebook configuration, the parent-report template revision, the PLC-minutes template revision, the classroom-walkthrough form revision, and the student-reflection prompt set.

## Step 2 — The seven descriptor-authoring teams draft behavioral descriptors for Bilingual Communicators, Compassionate, Engaged, Innovative, Reflective, and Responsible

**Description**

The seven descriptor-authoring teams draft behavioral descriptors for Bilingual Communicators, Compassionate, Engaged, Innovative, Reflective, and Responsible at the depth of the existing Confident descriptor, with Confident carried forward unchanged as the reference. Each team includes faculty from the Bilingual Programme, the Chinese Department, the English Department, the Primary and Secondary subject departments, the Early Years team, the homeroom system, and the Co-Curricular Programme so that the descriptor language is operable across Chinese-medium and English-medium settings, across EY, Primary, and Secondary, and across academic and co-curricular contexts. The Vice Principal for Academics and the Vice Principal for Bilingual Programme co-edit the seven drafts into a single descriptor set that is internally consistent in voice, depth, and observable-behavior framing, cross-references the three mission areas of Academic excellence, Caring and safe community, and Global citizenship, and is mapped to WASC standards A1, A4, B1, B2, B3, and C1. The Heads of Early Years, Primary, and Secondary review the consolidated draft for divisional fit and the Senior Leadership Team approves it for publication to stakeholders for feedback.

**Assessment**

The consolidated initial-draft descriptor set covers all seven SLOs at the depth of the Confident descriptor, carries a mapping table from each SLO to the mission areas and to WASC A1, A4, B1, B2, B3, and C1, and is signed off by the Vice Principal for Academics, the Vice Principal for Bilingual Programme, the Heads of Early Years, Primary, and Secondary, and the Principal in the SLT minutes.

## Step 3 — The Head of Admissions and Communications, working with the Vice Principal for Academics, publicizes the initial draft to all five impacted stakeholder groups

**Description**

The Head of Admissions and Communications, working with the Vice Principal for Academics, publicizes the initial draft to all five impacted stakeholder groups along the channel each group already uses. Staff and Faculty receive the draft in a whole-staff briefing followed by structured feedback in each division's PLC and in the Bilingual Programme cross-divisional meeting, with a written response form due within two weeks. Students receive a division-appropriate version through homeroom in Primary and Secondary and through EY teacher-led conversations, with student-voice feedback collected by the Head of Student Affairs in age-appropriate formats. Parents receive the draft through the parent reporting portal and a parent-evening presentation in each division, with written feedback collected through the portal. The Parent Committee receives the draft in a dedicated session with the Principal and the Head of Admissions and Communications and returns written commentary. Administration and Leadership review the draft in the SLT meeting that closes the consultation window. Each channel records its feedback in a single consultation log organized by SLO and by stakeholder group.

**Assessment**

The consultation log shows feedback received from each of the five stakeholder groups (Administration / Leadership, Staff / Faculty, Students, Parents, and Parent Committee) on each of the seven SLO descriptors, with timestamps and the channel of origin recorded for each entry, and the Head of Admissions and Communications confirms in writing that every division and the Bilingual Programme have returned faculty feedback and that every division has returned student feedback.

## Step 4 — The seven descriptor-authoring teams reconvene under the Vice Principal for Academics and the Vice Principal for Bilingual Programme to integrate the consultation feedback

**Description**

The seven descriptor-authoring teams reconvene under the Vice Principal for Academics and the Vice Principal for Bilingual Programme to integrate the consultation feedback into a revised descriptor set. Each team works through its SLO's feedback entries in the consultation log, records a disposition for each entry (incorporated, partially incorporated with rationale, not incorporated with rationale), and revises the descriptor accordingly so that the resulting language is operable across the Bilingual Programme, the subject departments, the homeroom system, and the Co-Curricular Programme, and across EY, Primary, and Secondary. The Heads of Early Years, Primary, and Secondary review the revised set for divisional fit and the Head of Student Affairs reviews it for student-facing clarity. The disposition table and the revised descriptor set are presented to the Senior Leadership Team for the finalization decision.

**Assessment**

The disposition table records a disposition with rationale for every consultation-log entry across all seven SLOs and all five stakeholder groups, the revised descriptor set retains the depth benchmark of the Confident descriptor, and the Vice Principal for Academics, the Vice Principal for Bilingual Programme, the Heads of Early Years, Primary, and Secondary, and the Head of Student Affairs sign the revised set as ready for SLT finalization.

## Step 5 — The Senior Leadership Team finalizes the revised descriptor set by 2025-11-28, the Principal authorizes publication

**Description**

The Senior Leadership Team finalizes the revised descriptor set by 2025-11-28, the Principal authorizes publication, and the Head of Admissions and Communications publishes the seven descriptors as a single canonical artifact in the staff handbook, the student-facing learning materials in the appropriate division-level form, the parent handbook, the parent reporting portal, and the school website's accreditation section. Publication includes the SLO-to-mission-area mapping and the SLO-to-WASC-standard mapping (A1, A4, B1, B2, B3, C1) so that downstream artifact owners and the self-study evidence binder draw on the same source of truth. The Vice Principal for Academics issues the version-stamped descriptor set to the unit-template owner, the gradebook configurator, the parent-report-template owner, the PLC-minutes template owner, the classroom-walkthrough form owner, and the student-reflection prompt owner as the input to the propagation step.

**Assessment**

The seven finalized SLO descriptors with the mission-area and WASC-standard mapping are published, version-stamped, and dated on or before 2025-11-28 in the staff handbook, the parent handbook, the parent reporting portal, the student-facing learning materials in each division's form, and the school website's accreditation section, and the version-stamped artifact is in the hands of each downstream-artifact owner with receipt confirmed in writing.

## Step 6 — The downstream-artifact owners embed the finalized SLO descriptors into the operating instruments of the school

**Description**

The downstream-artifact owners embed the finalized SLO descriptors into the operating instruments of the school so that the SLOs become the working language for learning across all four framing audiences and across every division for the remainder of the 2025-2026 cycle. The Vice Principal for Academics revises the unit-planning template to require one or two SLOs per unit and at least one SLO-tagged assessment artifact per unit, and rolls the revised template into the Term 2 unit-planning round in Early Years, Primary, and Secondary and in the Bilingual Programme. The gradebook configurator adds the SLO field at the assessment level and configures the report generator to emit an SLO-organized parent narrative section at each reporting cycle. The PLC-minutes template owner revises the template so that every PLC agenda opens with an SLO frame and the closing item records SLO-tagged evidence of student learning, and the Heads of Early Years, Primary, and Secondary direct their division's PLC chairs to use the revised template from the next cycle. The classroom-walkthrough form owner reorganizes the form so that walkthrough notes are captured under the seven SLOs as the organizing categories. The Head of Student Affairs publishes the student-reflection prompt set for term reporting and portfolio review in age-appropriate forms for EY, Primary, and Secondary. The Head of Co-Curricular Programme adds the SLO frame to the co-curricular planning and recognition routines so that Compassionate, Responsible, and Engaged are evidenced in co-curricular settings. The Head of Admissions and Communications updates the parent-evening agenda template and the admissions-information materials so that the SLO vocabulary is the through-line. The Senior Leadership Team schedules the self-study evidence binder's construction against WASC A1, A4, B1, B2, B3, and C1 using the seven SLOs as the organizing evidence categories, with the binder due 2026-07-24.

**Assessment**

The Term 2 unit-planning round shows the revised template in use across Early Years, Primary, Secondary, and the Bilingual Programme; the gradebook carries the SLO field at the assessment level and the report generator produces the SLO-organized parent narrative section at each reporting cycle; the PLC-minutes template is in use in every division with opening SLO frame and closing SLO-tagged evidence; the classroom-walkthrough form is organized by SLO; the student-reflection prompt set is published in division-appropriate forms; the co-curricular planning and recognition routines record SLO evidence; the parent-evening agenda template and admissions-information materials carry the SLO vocabulary; and the self-study evidence binder's construction plan against A1, A4, B1, B2, B3, and C1 organized by the seven SLOs is on the SLT calendar with the 2026-07-24 due date.
