# Policy revision (6-step consultative)

## Step 1 — The Director of Teaching and Curriculum Center presents options at an admin meeting

**Description**

The Director of Teaching and Curriculum Center presents options at an admin meeting convened with the IHS Principal, the Director of AAO, the Director of Student Affairs Office, and the Director of Future Scholar Innovation Center for authoring shared behavioral descriptors for the six undefined Schoolwide Learner Outcomes (Bilingual Communicators, Compassionate, Engaged, Innovative, Reflective, Responsible) at the depth of the existing Confident descriptor. Options cover descriptor-authoring sequencing, cross-divisional faculty team composition drawing on Heads of English, Humanities, Math, Sciences, Art Department, Business and Economics, Music and Sports Department, and the College Counseling Department Coordinator, validation pathway through divisional leaders, and the downstream embedding scope into unit-plan templates, PLC agendas, term-reporting templates, portfolio-review rubrics, classroom walkthrough instruments, and the WASC self-study evidence binder organized against standards A1, A4, B1, B2, B3, and C1.

**Assessment**

Admin meeting minutes recorded by the IHS Assistant show the options were presented and a single option was selected for advancement; the selected option is on file in the Principal's Office (PO).

## Step 2 — The Director of Teaching and Curriculum Center, supported by the Senior Teaching Coordinator and the PD Leader, finalizes an initial draft

**Description**

The Director of Teaching and Curriculum Center, supported by the Senior Teaching Coordinator and the PD Leader, finalizes an initial draft of the seven SLO behavioral descriptor set. The draft is authored by cross-divisional faculty teams pairing the Head of English, Head of Humanities, Head of Math, Head of Sciences, Head of Art Department, Head of Business and Economics, and a Music Teacher and PE Teacher from the Music and Sports Department, with each team writing one descriptor for an unwritten SLO using the existing Confident descriptor as the depth model; the Homeroom Teacher cohort under the Director of Student Affairs Office contributes pastoral framing for Compassionate, Responsible, and Engaged; the College Counseling Department Coordinator contributes outcome framing tied to onward pathways; the Librarian contributes language tied to information-literacy expression of Reflective and Innovative; and the Director of Future Scholar Innovation Center contributes framing for Innovative. The draft also includes companion language for the unit-plan template, the PLC agenda frame, the term-reporting SLO narrative section, and the portfolio-review reflection prompts.

**Assessment**

A single initial draft document containing the seven descriptors and the companion language is held by the Director of Teaching and Curriculum Center and circulated to the IHS Principal for the consultation stage.

## Step 3 — The IHS Principal and the Director of Teaching and Curriculum Center publicize the initial draft to stakeholders

**Description**

The IHS Principal and the Director of Teaching and Curriculum Center publicize the initial draft to stakeholders for feedback. Staff / Faculty receive the draft through division-led review sessions run by the Head of English, Head of Humanities, Head of Math, Head of Sciences, Head of Art Department, Head of Business and Economics, and the Music and Sports Department, with the AAO - Academic Affairs Office circulating the draft to all teaching staff via the Academic Affairs Officer. Students receive the draft through Homeroom Teacher sessions coordinated by the A2 Year Leader, AS Year Leader, IG1 Year Leader, and IG2 Year Leader under the Director of Student Affairs Office, with response gathered in homeroom. Parents and the Parent Committee receive the draft through a written communication issued from the Principal's Office (PO) and a feedback session convened by the IHS Principal. Administration / Leadership review the draft through the existing leadership meeting cadence chaired by the IHS Principal. The College Counselor circulates the draft to current Admissions Applicants families through the College Counseling Department for forward-looking visibility.

**Assessment**

Feedback intake log maintained by the IHS Assistant records receipt of written and meeting-captured feedback from Staff / Faculty (by division), Students (by year), Parents, the Parent Committee, and Administration / Leadership, with date and channel for each entry.

## Step 4 — The Director of Teaching and Curriculum Center, with the Senior Teaching Coordinator and the PD Leader, integrates the feedback

**Description**

The Director of Teaching and Curriculum Center, with the Senior Teaching Coordinator and the PD Leader, integrates the feedback into a revised descriptor set and the companion language for unit-plan templates, PLC agenda frames, term-reporting SLO narrative sections, and portfolio-review reflection prompts. Each Head of Department (Head of English, Head of Humanities, Head of Math, Head of Sciences, Head of Art Department, Head of Business and Economics) signs off on the revisions affecting their department's unit-plan implications; the Director of Student Affairs Office signs off on revisions to descriptors tied to Compassionate, Responsible, and Engaged and to homeroom-facing student reflection language; the Director of AAO signs off on the gradebook-field and reporting-template language to be carried by the AAO - Academic Affairs Office; the Director of Future Scholar Innovation Center signs off on Innovative; and the College Counseling Department Coordinator signs off on outcome framing tied to onward pathways.

**Assessment**

Revision tracking document maintained by the Director of Teaching and Curriculum Center shows each feedback item received in step 3 and its disposition (incorporated, modified, or declined with stated reason), with departmental and divisional sign-offs recorded against the revised draft.

## Step 5 — The IHS Principal finalizes the revised SLO descriptor set after feedback and publishes it

**Description**

The IHS Principal finalizes the revised SLO descriptor set after feedback and publishes it. Publication channels include the staff handbook (maintained by the Principal's Office (PO) with distribution support from the IHS Assistant), the student-facing learning materials (distributed by Homeroom Teachers across all year groups under the Director of Student Affairs Office), and the parent reporting portal and parent handbook (issued by the Principal's Office (PO)). The AAO - Academic Affairs Office, under the Director of AAO, publishes the descriptors into the unit-plan template repository and the gradebook reference fields. The Librarian deposits a reference copy in the Library collection.

**Assessment**

Published copies of the seven SLO behavioral descriptors are present in the staff handbook, the parent handbook, the parent reporting portal, the unit-plan template repository, the gradebook reference, and the Library collection, verified by the IHS Assistant against a publication checklist held in the Principal's Office (PO).

## Step 6 — The Director of Teaching and Curriculum Center propagates and embeds the published descriptors into downstream artifacts

**Description**

The Director of Teaching and Curriculum Center propagates and embeds the published descriptors into downstream artifacts so the descriptors operate as the school's working language for learning. The unit-plan template carried by the AAO - Academic Affairs Office requires every department's units for the 2025-2026 cycle to name the one or two SLOs each unit advances and to attach at least one SLO-tagged assessment artifact, with the Senior Teaching Coordinator and the PD Leader supporting each Head of Department (Head of English, Head of Humanities, Head of Math, Head of Sciences, Head of Art Department, Head of Business and Economics) in embedding the descriptors into their department's unit plans. PLC cycles in every division open with an SLO frame and close with SLO-tagged evidence in PLC minutes, coordinated by the PD Leader. Term reporting and portfolio review, administered through the AAO - Academic Affairs Office and Homeroom Teachers under the Director of Student Affairs Office across all year groups led by the A2 Year Leader, AS Year Leader, IG1 Year Leader, and IG2 Year Leader, carry an SLO-organized parent narrative and an SLO student self-reflection. Classroom walkthroughs and work-sample reviews conducted by the IHS Principal, the Director of Teaching and Curriculum Center, and each Head of Department are organized by SLO. The annual self-study against WASC standards A1, A4, B1, B2, B3, and C1 is compiled by the Director of Teaching and Curriculum Center with the IHS Principal, using the seven SLOs as the organizing evidence categories and drawing on SLO-tagged unit plans, PLC minutes, walkthrough notes, work-sample reviews, student reflections, and parent reports produced during the cycle. The Class Schedule Officer in the AAO - Academic Affairs Office ensures PLC time is held in the schedule. The Director of Future Scholar Innovation Center embeds the Innovative descriptor into Future Scholar Innovation Center programs, and the College Counseling Department uses the descriptor set in counseling conversations with students.

**Assessment**

End-of-cycle inspection by the IHS Principal and the Director of Teaching and Curriculum Center confirms: 100 percent of departmental units in the 2025-2026 cycle have unit plans naming advancing SLO(s) and linking at least one SLO-tagged assessment artifact (verified against the unit-plan repository held by the AAO - Academic Affairs Office); at least 90 percent of logged PLC cycles across all divisions show both opening SLO frame and closing SLO-tagged evidence in their minutes; the SLO narrative section is present and populated in parent reports for every enrolled student at each reporting cycle; student SLO self-reflections are on file from term reporting and portfolio review for every enrolled student; and the WASC self-study evidence binder for standards A1, A4, B1, B2, B3, and C1 is organized by the seven SLOs with citations to the cycle's artifacts.
