## Structure of the frame (four nested layers)
1. **Values = the ethos** (Respect/Wholeness/Truth/Excellence) — *how* the school acts; the substrate beneath
   the outcomes, not measured monthly.
2. **SLOs = three developmental DIMENSIONS** (Grounded/Rounded/Unbounded), each with sub-traits — 8 sub-trait
   strands (3 + 3 + 2) — **PLUS the retained cross-cutting SLO Bilingual Communicators** (Chinese + English,
   present verbatim in Vision + Mission). **9 measured strands total.** What students "become more and more."
3. **Vision = the school we seek**; **Mission = our commitment + the means** (high expectations, guide + support).
   Vision and Mission are parallel: they enumerate the *same* outcome set; Mission adds the "how."

## Bilingual Communicators — modeling rules (2026-06-09 refinement)
How the bilingual strand must be modeled in the sim (and eventually the workshopping). Corrects the run-001 SAO
arcs that used "alternate Chinese/English by week" — that is WRONG.
- **Simultaneous, never alternating.** English is pushed/used alongside Chinese in *every* relevant interaction —
  not "Chinese weeks / English weeks." The practice is always-on English use, scaffolded, not a rotation.
- **Shared responsibility — NOT the English department's alone.** Responsibility for English improvement rests
  across the school; SAO and every out-of-lesson actor (HRT, Year Leaders, Dorm Teacher) own a real share,
  because most informal language happens outside lessons. The English dept supports, it does not solely own.
- **English-dept coordination is an EXAMPLE sub-action, NOT a fixed rule.** One concrete way the shared
  responsibility *can* be enacted: the English department supplies monthly vocab lists + sentence
  starters/skeletons, practiced out-of-lesson and tracked/assessed by SAO. This is a candidate sub-action item
  the process MAY generate — not mandatory; other concrete forms are equally valid. (The binding rules are the
  other bullets in this list; this one is illustrative of a sub-action need.)
- **Measure STAFF behavior, not only student outcomes.** A primary bilingual instrument is a **staff survey of
  how much English adults actually use / nudge outside lessons** — the adult behavior that drives student English
  use. Example items (verbatim):
  > When speaking with students outside of lessons, what percentage of the time do you:
  > - Nudge students to communicate in English as best they can?
  > - Respond in English, regardless of the language the student speaks in?
- **Workshops embody English-improvement pedagogy.** SAO's SLO workshops are themselves run as
  English-improvement best practice (comprehensible input, the sentence skeletons in use, scaffolded output) — the
  medium models the message: an SLO workshop is also an English-practice vehicle.

Implication for the arc shape: the Bilingual strand's arcs carry BOTH a student-progress measure (against the
English-dept vocab/skeletons, tracked by SAO) AND a staff-behavior measure (the English-usage survey) — re-run on
the spiral cadence (the quarter-ends (Q1≈end-Oct · Q2≈end-Jan · Q3≈end-Apr · Q4=Jun)). It ties to the run-001 finding "two kinds of target": here, staff-behavior
instruments + student-outcome instruments, both required.

## The SPIRAL skeleton (dominant focus + retrieval practice = durable mastery)
The pedagogical principle the user set: a dominant monthly focus, BUT retrieval practice of all prior strands so
the whole are durably mastered — never "next… and abandon prior." Measurement is cumulative: baseline at launch →
deep measure when dominant → re-check in every later spiral → year-end all-strand re-measure. Three "movements"
(one per dimension), bookended by baseline-launch and synthesis-close, with a mid-year Grounded retrieval beat.

| Month | Dominant strand | Spiral retrieval (re-practice + re-measure priors) |
|---|---|---|
| Aug–Sep | **Launch — baseline ALL 9 strands** (8 sub-traits + Bilingual Communicators) | establish starting points across all dimensions + bilingual |
| Oct | Grounded · Emotionally resilient | — |
| Nov | Grounded · Physically robust | + emotionally resilient |
| Dec | Grounded · Spiritually alive & happy · **Grounded synthesis** | + prior Grounded strands; **Bilingual re-measure** |
| Jan | Rounded · Politically informed | **+ Grounded retrieval checkpoint** |
| Feb | Rounded · Environmentally aware · **Bilingual Communicators spotlight** | + Grounded |
| Mar | Rounded · Technologically fluent · **Rounded synthesis** | + Grounded + Rounded priors; **Bilingual re-measure** |
| Apr | Unbounded · Intellectually curious / internationally minded | + Grounded + Rounded |
| May | Unbounded · Seeking opportunity / finding achievement · **portfolios** | + all priors; **Bilingual re-measure** |
| Jun | **Synthesis — re-measure ALL 9 strands; report; revise** | full retrieval; durable-mastery check |

**Bilingual Communicators** is woven EVERY month as the Chinese+English medium (always practiced), gets its own
dominant **spotlight (Feb)**, and is **re-measured at every synthesis** (the quarter-ends (Q1≈end-Oct · Q2≈end-Jan · Q3≈end-Apr · Q4=Jun)) — a first-class strand,
never dropped. The **Values** thread (Respect/Wholeness/Truth/Excellence) runs under every month as the ethos.

The arc per division (DEC-51, sharpened): baseline → adjust → measure → **spiral-retrieve → re-measure**, scoped
to the month's dominant strand, with Bilingual (measured) and the Values (ethos) woven through.

### Quarterly evaluate-and-revise cadence + GROWTH-MINDSET planning (2026-06-09)
The year divides into FOUR QUARTERS, and the quarter boundaries are the **evaluate-and-(possibly)-revise** cycle
points — NOT a first review only at Dec (half the year is too late to learn we're off-track).
- **Q1 Aug–Oct (close ≈ end-Oct)** · **Q2 Nov–Jan (≈ end-Jan)** · **Q3 Feb–Apr (≈ end-Apr)** · **Q4 May–Jun (Jun, year-end).**
  The first checkpoint lands **before Dec** by design, so course-correction is possible early.
- **At each quarter close, the same loop runs (all three tracks):** MEASURE → **are we moving the needles as we
  intended?** → if not, **WHY** (diagnose root cause, not just note the miss) → **what ADJUSTMENTS** can we make
  (to actions, owners, resources, or the target itself) → revise and continue. Q4 = full re-measure → annual
  report → revise → **seeds next year's baseline.**
- **Growth-mindset in planning:** the plan is a hypothesis tested quarterly, not a fixed commitment graded at the
  end. Mid-course correction is expected and designed-in — a quarter that reveals "not moving as intended, here's
  why, here's the adjustment" is the system working, not failing. The same growth orientation the SLOs ask of
  students is applied to the planning itself.
- The monthly dominant-strand spiral continues underneath; the quarter close is where measurement becomes
  judgment + revision. Replaces the earlier Dec-first re-measure rhythm.

## Two measurement tracks — student AND faculty/staff (both year-long)
Every strand is evidenced on TWO parallel tracks, each baselined at Launch, re-measured on the spiral cadence
(the quarter-ends (Q1≈end-Oct · Q2≈end-Jan · Q3≈end-Apr · Q4=Jun)), and providing evidence over time:
- **Student track** — student-outcome growth on the strand (surveys, observations, portfolios, student work/artifacts).
- **Faculty/staff track** — the ADULT behavior that drives the student outcome (e.g. the bilingual staff
  English-usage survey; staff adoption of a routine; PD uptake; how often staff model/nudge the strand). The
  bilingual staff-usage survey is the first instance of this track — it generalizes: most strands have a staff/
  faculty behavior that should be baselined + measured, not only the student outcome.
Each arc therefore carries (where applicable) BOTH a student measure and a faculty/staff measure. PO aggregates
both tracks into the SLO-Implementation Report; the two tracks together are the evidence the plan ran durably.

## Stakeholders as a MEASURED dimension — parents especially (the third track)
Stakeholders are not only an audience to report to (that is "Stakeholder communications", below) — their
**awareness, understanding, and engagement** with the school's mission/vision/SLOs is itself a MEASURED outcome.
A school that has genuinely embedded its SLOs has *parents who know them*. This is the THIRD measurement track,
alongside the student track and the faculty/staff track.
- **Parents are a key stakeholder and a catalogued group** — the tenant has both `Parents` and a `Parent
  Committee` stakeholder group (verified). The Parent Committee is a two-way PARTNER channel, not broadcast-only.
- **Measure parent awareness/understanding/engagement with mission/vision/SLOs:** baseline at Launch (do parents
  know the mission, the vision, the SLOs; can they name what the school is developing in their child) → **mid-year
  and end-of-year targets** (baseline + [DM-INPUT] increment) → **standing quarterly check** on the existing
  re-measure beats (the quarter-ends (Q1≈end-Oct · Q2≈end-Jan · Q3≈end-Apr · Q4=Jun)) → the cycle's results seed next year's baseline.
- **Instrument:** a parent awareness/engagement survey (run via Parents + the Parent Committee), plus
  Parent-Committee partnership signals. Quarterly cadence (the tenant frequency vocab is Annually/Termly/Twice-a-
  year/Monthly — the four re-measure beats give the de-facto quarterly rhythm; a `Quarterly` frequency may be
  added to the catalogue).
- **Generalizes:** other stakeholders (community, alumni, board) can be measured the same way where warranted;
  parents are the primary instance. Note: tenant `feedback_channels` are currently UNSEEDED (0) — a precursor:
  the parent/stakeholder feedback channels must be stood up before the IN measurement can run.
- This makes stakeholder engagement a tri-track outcome (student · staff · stakeholder/parent), each baselined +
  re-measured quarterly + seeding the next cycle.

## Stakeholder communications (a first-class element)
The plan must COMMUNICATE with stakeholders — parents, students, faculty/staff, the wider community (and board) —
both OUT and IN:
- **Out (report):** progress communicated on the spiral cadence (synthesis points = communication points) —
  newsletters, parent sessions, assemblies, the published SLO report. PO owns the schoolwide report; each
  division communicates its own arc's progress. Maps to the canonical "Communication of Progress" column.
- **In (gather):** stakeholder feedback collected and fed into the revision loop (the measure→data→revise cycle) —
  parent/student/staff feedback surveys; maps to feedback_channels + the stakeholder coverage-floor.
Stakeholder communications is two-way, on the spiral cadence, and is both a deliverable (transparency) and a
measurement input (feedback drives revision). Each arc/synthesis names how + to whom progress is communicated and
what feedback is gathered.

## Global thinking framework (the process spine these sims refine — 2026-06-09)

### The fractal / holographic principle (FOUNDATIONAL — corrects strand-ownership)
**Each shard contains and IS the whole, in its dimension.** The plan's whole — all 9 SLO strands + the vision +
the mission + the values — is present in EVERY division, refracted through that division's purview/dimension. A
division does NOT "own" a subset of strands; it engages ALL of them through its lens.
- College Counseling is NOT "the Unbounded owner." It refracts the whole through college-counseling: Grounded (the
  resilient student in the application-stress crucible), Rounded (globally/politically informed pathways),
  Unbounded (opportunity/achievement), Bilingual (application English), the values — all nine, its way.
- Same for every division: SAO refracts all nine through pastoral/out-of-lesson; C&T through curriculum/teaching;
  Math through mathematical practice; PO through governance. The whole, everywhere, differently.
- **This corrects "distribute ownership across divisions."** Distribution is NOT "assign strand X to division A"
  (that re-introduces concentration at the strand level — the foreclosure error in runs 003/004 where divisions
  were told which strands they "own"). Distribution is: the whole refracted through every shard's dimension.
- **It recurses (fractal):** division → position (each seat refracts the division's whole), strand → sub-traits,
  year → spiral months, plan → arc. Each scale reproduces the whole in its dimension.
- Practical brief rule: never tell a division which strands it owns. Ask it to refract the WHOLE (all 9 + vision/
  mission/values) through its own dimension — surfacing where its lens makes each strand vivid (and saying so when
  its refraction of a given strand is faint, rather than ignoring it).


### Plan-cycle start sequence — MANDATORY front-load (every cycle, before any action)
A plan cycle always begins with these, in order, BEFORE proposing actions:
1. **Baseline establishment** — where the division/strand actually starts (both tracks: student + faculty/staff).
2. **Articulate how progress + success will be measured** — name the instruments + cadence + what "success"
   looks like, on BOTH tracks, up front (not after the action is chosen).
3. **Identify precursor requirements / dependencies** — what must be in place first (data, tools, training,
   coordination, another division's output, a policy) for the work to be possible. If none, say so.
Only after 1–3 does the cycle propose action. This front-load is the discipline that makes progress durable +
measurable — you cannot improve what you have not baselined and defined success for.

### Options posture — subordinate provides OPTIONS; decision-maker decides
The system's organizational role is a **subordinate that supplies exceedingly well-thought-out OPTIONS** for the
human decision-maker (the school) to choose from / synthesize — NOT to pre-decide.
- LLMs are strong at generating options; lean into it. For each action decision point, present a **curated set of
  distinct, well-reasoned options** — each with its rationale + trade-offs + the arc shape (baseline→adjust→
  measure, dual tracks, comms).
- Options need **NOT be mutually exclusive** — the decision-maker may pick one, several, or synthesize.
- **Without cognitive overload** — a FEW strong, genuinely distinct options per decision point (not a sprawling
  menu); curated and ranked/annotated so the decision-maker can decide efficiently.
- The decision-maker decides; the system frames the decision well. (NB: this is the PLAN-CONTENT posture for the
  generated plan — distinct from the orchestrator's own anti-options mandate when advising on THIS project's build.)

### Articulate the need — DECISION REQUESTS (upward complement to options; 2026-06-09)
Where a plan element cannot be completed without a decision or input the subordinate does NOT own — a monthly/term
growth target (the DM-set increment over baseline), a precursor leadership/PO decision, a resource, a policy
authorization, or another division's output — the subordinate must NOT silently skip or leave a bare placeholder.
It articulates an explicit **DECISION REQUEST** stating:
- **What** is needed (the specific decision/input).
- **From whom** (PO / school leadership / board / a named other division).
- **Why** it is required (what plan element depends on it).
- **What it blocks / unblocks** (what cannot proceed until it is decided).
- **(where useful)** a curated option-set or a recommendation for that decision, so the decider can act efficiently.
This makes the human-input boundary a first-class, actionable surface, not a silent gap. The options posture is
the DOWNWARD flow (subordinate → DM: here are choices); the decision request is the UPWARD flow (subordinate → DM:
here is what I need you to decide so I can proceed). PO consolidates decision requests into the decision brief
alongside the options, sequenced by what they block.

### Science-of-learning as an OPTION-DESIGN DISCIPLINE — every contact = an effective lesson element (2026-06-09)
Science-of-learning is not only a possible THEME (run-007); it is a **standing design discipline on the options
themselves**, applied to EVERY plan whatever its theme (alongside catalogue-fidelity DEC-40 + the register
rules). A plan action is an intervention meant to produce durable change; an action *designed as transmission*
fails ("talking at" produces neither durable learning nor mission/vision/SLO advancement). So every option a
division generates is a **learning-designed intervention**.

**The CONTACT-TYPE STRUCTURE (mandatory shape of every contact — lesson, HRT/YGL workshop, meeting, counseling
session, assembly, PD).** Every contact is structured exactly as an effective lesson element:
1. **OPEN** — activation + **measurement of prior knowledge** + **retrieval** (recall, not re-tell).
2. **CORE** — a **genuine generative task**, chosen from a **VARIETY of generative-task options** — the best fit
   selected for the *specific instance* (not one default). (A generative-task menu exists: think-pair-share,
   retrieval-write/brain-dump, build/critique, problem-solving, role-play, peer-teach, debate, produce-an-
   artifact, sort/rank, etc. — selection-for-fit is required, not a single mandated task.)
3. **EVIDENCE** — **evidence of understanding** (a check that surfaces where participants actually are).
4. **CLOSE** — **reflection / next steps**.
This arc structurally precludes "talking-at" (it requires activation, generation, evidence, and reflection) and
makes spacing/feedback/metacognition native. It is the shape EVERY option's action must take.

**Option-element block (each option carries these, refracted per division's medium):** active/generative core ·
retrieval + spacing · feedback loop (formative, acted-on) · metacognition · load (chunked/sustainable) ·
**durability evidence** (a delayed measure — did it STICK — not occurrence).

**Option-quality gate (at generation):** (a) **no transmission-only option** — a pure lecture/assembly/memo/
"talk-at" option is flagged low-quality and redesigned to the contact-type structure; (b) **each option SET must
contain ≥1 genuinely active/generative option** (a set of transmission variants fails); (c) measure **durability,
not occurrence**.

**Per-division refraction (fractal):** the contact-arc + elements are uniform; each division instantiates them in
its medium — C&T in lessons, SAO in HRT/YGL workshops/assemblies, College Counseling in counseling/info-evenings,
PO in meetings/PD. The **self-refutation guard** applies: staff-facing contacts (PD, training, meetings) follow
the same contact-type structure — measured on the same instrument; PO fixes itself first.

**Where it plugs in (no new machinery):** front-load carries the "talking-at %" baseline; generation makes every
option carry the element block + the contact-type structure + pass the gate; the decision-brief ranks options
partly on science-of-learning quality; measurement requires the durability (delayed) read.

**RENDER RULE — embody, don't label (generation-side vs the artifact).** The contact-type structure, the
element block, the method-selection reasoning, and the framework vocabulary ("talking-at %", "self-refutation
guard", "contact-type", "durability-not-occurrence", "tracks") are GENERATION-SIDE — how we author and choose a
good step. The RENDERED plan must EMBODY the method in plain plan prose — a step that opens by recalling prior
work, runs a real generative task, checks understanding, and closes with reflection — and must NOT show the
scaffolding: no OPEN/CORE/EVIDENCE/CLOSE labels, no "(why this task)" methodology justifications, no
how-to-read/methodology panels. A real school plan contains well-designed action steps, not a description of its
own pedagogy framework; the science is visible because the steps ARE well-designed, not because they are labelled.
(run-008's first HTML over-labelled with the scaffolding; corrected to embodiment.) Same applies to the meta rows
(Measurement Tools, Success criteria, Decisions): plain plan language, not framework terms.

**The governing rule (sharpened by the user 2026-06-09):** framework/process vocabulary does not belong in a
planning artifact AT ALL, anywhere in it. The invented method terms (self-refutation guard, refraction, fractal,
weave, front-load, contact-type structure, "talking-at %", durability-not-occurrence, behavior-change-primary,
options posture, evaluate-and-revise cadence, growth-mindset loop, keystone, precursor, cross-track) live ONLY in
these sim-land records, never in a rendered plan. This is DISTINCT from plain-diction: ordinary descriptive English
is fine even when vivid — "talking at students" is fine, it plainly names a real thing; only the coined framework
terms are banned. ("self-refutation guard" was the AI's coinage, not the user's — never attribute method names to
the user.) Replace with plain equivalents in the artifact (the common measure = "how much of the time the adult is
talking versus students are working"; staff sessions "run the same way as good lessons, and leadership holds its
own meetings to the same standard"). All three companion plan HTMLs (environment, SLO-embedding, science-of-
learning) were rewritten to this rule 2026-06-09.

### Findings adjustments (from run-002 — fold into every subsequent sim)
- **Staff-track: separate compliance from behavior-change.** A `[STAFF]` measure must distinguish *did-they-do-it*
  (compliance: % who ran the routine) from *did-their-practice-shift* (behavior-change: the actual adult-behavior
  delta — the English-usage survey is the model). Prefer behavior-change as the primary staff measure.
- **Targets reference the baseline; the increment is a decision-maker input.** Do not invent target numbers; a
  target is "baseline + an increment the decision-maker sets." Mark the increment as a DM input, not an assumed value.
- **Consolidate stakeholder communications.** Comms is ONE coherent plan (channels + audiences + a feedback
  calendar) produced at synthesis — not formulaic per-arc repetition.
