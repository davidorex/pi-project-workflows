# Schoolwide SLO Plan — Executive Draft (rendered from sim run-005)

*A simulated, illustrative draft (sim-land; baselines are placeholders, targets await leadership increments).
The plan embeds the school's nine learner-outcome strands into daily practice across the school, by having
**every division refract the whole through its own work** — woven into one schoolwide picture per strand.*

## Frame
- **Theme:** Embed the SLOs into every element of the school's daily practice.
- **Outcomes (9 strands):** **Grounded** — Emotionally resilient · Physically robust · Spiritually alive & happy. **Rounded** — Politically informed · Environmentally aware · Technologically fluent. **Unbounded** — Intellectually curious & internationally minded · Seeking opportunity & finding achievement. **Bilingual Communicators** (Chinese + English, always-on).
- **Values (ethos):** Respect · Wholeness · Truth · Excellence. **Vision/Mission:** the plan must visibly *advance the vision* and *vivify the mission* (engaged, potential-realizing, life-long learners; responsible; self-esteeming; bilingual; compassionate).
- **How to read it:** each strand is one schoolwide capability woven from several divisions' facets — no single division "owns" a strand; where no division strongly delivers a strand, that is flagged as a gap.

## Cycle start — done FIRST, before any action (the front-load)
1. **Baselines** — every strand gets a starting read on **three tracks**: a *student* outcome read, a *faculty/staff behavior* read, and a *stakeholder/parent* read (do parents know the mission/vision/SLOs). No strand proceeds half-baselined.
2. **Measurement defined up front** — for each strand: the instrument, the cadence (re-measured each quarter-end (Q1≈end-Oct · Q2≈end-Jan · Q3≈end-Apr · Q4=Jun)), and what success means. Staff measures distinguish *compliance* (did the routine run) from *behavior-change* (did adult practice actually shift — the primary signal). Targets = *baseline + an increment leadership sets* (not invented).
3. **Precursors cleared** — each action's prerequisites (data system, training, tools, policy, coordination) named with an owner + ready-by date before launch.

## The plan, by strand (the refraction weave)
Each strand = one schoolwide picture + the division facets that compose it. (●=vivid facet · ○=light/faint)

**Grounded · Emotionally resilient** — *the resilient student in lesson, dorm, and application is one student.*
- ● SAO: homeroom "reset" check-ins + dorm evening debrief + Year-Leader stage-arc (IG1 transition → A2 exam load). ● College Counseling: application-stress-crucible debrief + rejection-reframe protocol. ○ C&T: productive-struggle task framing (resilience as a by-product of lesson design).
- Measure: student coping self-report · staff "names/normalizes setback" behavior.

**Grounded · Physically robust** — ● SAO: dorm sleep + movement routine; homeroom move/fuel nudge. ○ C&T: movement-integrated lessons, cognitive-load-aware design. ○ College Counseling: workload sustainability surfaced in pathway-fit. *Thin outside SAO.*

**Grounded · Spiritually alive & happy** — ● SAO: dorm gratitude/reflection ritual; homeroom "what mattered this week." ● College Counseling: purpose/values-fit advising; fit-over-prestige framing. ○ C&T: meaning/wonder in curriculum.
- Measure: student belonging/happiness anchor · staff warmth-of-facilitation.

**Rounded · Politically informed** — ● C&T: discipline-embedded current-affairs inquiry + structured-deliberation protocol. ● SAO: house-voice/student-rep with closed-loop ("my view counts") + respectful-disagreement norm. ● College Counseling: socially/globally-aware destination choice (geopolitics, civic context).
- Measure: student reasoned-argument/perspective-taking · staff facilitation + % of student-raised items closed-loop.

**Rounded · Environmentally aware** — ● C&T: sustainability lens in units + Feb interdisciplinary eco-project. ● SAO: dorm/house sustainability habits (waste/energy/water). ○ College Counseling: values-in-pathway-choice when student-raised.

**Rounded · Technologically fluent** — ● C&T: embedded-tech task design + digital-pedagogy PD ladder. ● College Counseling: research databases + application-portal fluency. ○ SAO: digital-citizenship/device habits only. **⚠ COVERAGE GAP: no division vividly delivers tech-*skill* itself** — needs a lead owner (see Decisions).
- Measure: student purposeful tool-use in work · staff purposeful-integration.

**Unbounded · Intellectually curious & internationally minded** — ● C&T: inquiry-default lesson architecture + international-mindedness in content selection. ● College Counseling: international-pathways horizon-expansion + curiosity-to-programme mapping. ● SAO: clubs / dorm "rabbit-hole" curiosity share.
- Measure: student question-generation/exploration · staff "widens rather than narrows."

**Unbounded · Seeking opportunity & finding achievement** — ● College Counseling: opportunity portfolio (competitions/programmes/scholarships) + achievement-evidencing for applications. ● C&T: portfolio-of-evidence + mastery/stretch task design. ● SAO: homeroom achievement mentoring + opportunity brokering. *(Equity note: proactively surface opportunities for lower-access students.)*
- Measure: opportunities pursued / portfolio breadth · staff opportunity-surfacing.

**Bilingual Communicators** *(always-on, simultaneous, all-staff — most-refracted strand)* — ● C&T: English-medium pedagogy in every lesson; PD embodies English pedagogy. ● SAO: out-of-lesson always-on English-nudge norm (most informal language is out-of-lesson — a real SAO share). ● College Counseling: application/interview/personal-statement English.
- Measure (primary, behavior): the **staff English-usage survey** — *"when speaking with students outside lessons, what % of the time do you (a) nudge students to use English, (b) respond in English regardless of the student's language"* — run across all staff; student bilingual-confidence read alongside. One consistent tone guardrail across all three facets (encouraging, never punitive).

**Values (Respect/Wholeness/Truth/Excellence)** — the ethos under every action: SAO is the home of Wholeness + Respect; C&T of Truth (assessment integrity) + Excellence (mastery); College Counseling of Truth (honest essays) + Respect (student-owned choice). Measured via one shared values instrument (to be set).

## The year — four quarters, each a quarterly evaluate-and-revise cycle (growth mindset)
The year runs in **four quarters**, and the quarter boundaries are the **evaluate-and-(possibly)-revise** points —
the first lands **before December** so we course-correct early: **Q1 Aug–Oct (≈ end-Oct) · Q2 Nov–Jan (≈ end-Jan)
· Q3 Feb–Apr (≈ end-Apr) · Q4 May–Jun (Jun, year-end → seeds next year).** At each quarter close: measure all
three tracks → *are we moving the needles as intended?* → if not, *why* (diagnose) → *what adjustments* (actions,
owners, resources, or the target) → revise. The plan is a hypothesis tested quarterly, not a fixed commitment
graded only at year-end — the same growth mindset the SLOs ask of students, applied to the planning itself.

## Underneath — the monthly dominant-strand spiral (dominant focus + retrieval → durable mastery)
Aug–Sep **Launch** (baseline all 9) · Oct Emotionally-resilient · Nov Physically-robust · Dec Spiritually-alive **+ Grounded synthesis** · Jan Politically-informed · Feb Environmentally-aware **+ Bilingual spotlight** · Mar Technologically-fluent **+ Rounded synthesis** · Apr Intellectually-curious · May Seeking-opportunity **+ portfolios** · Jun **Synthesis — re-measure all 9 → report → revise.** Bilingual runs every month; re-measured each quarter-end (Q1≈end-Oct · Q2≈end-Jan · Q3≈end-Apr · Q4=Jun). Each month spotlights one strand *and* retrieves the priors so none is dropped.

## Governance (Principal's Office)
PO delivers nothing directly; it governs every strand through six verbs — **baseline · measure · resource · hold accountable · report · revise** — and does the **weave** (consolidating each division's facets into one schoolwide picture per strand without flattening to a single owner). It runs the launch gates (baseline parity, measurement statements, precursor sweep), owns the single schoolwide communications + feedback plan, and runs the Jun re-measure → report → revise loop that opens next year.

## What leadership must decide (sequenced — the critical path)
*Presented as a short, ordered queue (not a sprawl); each is decide-once where possible.*
- **① Infrastructure (keystone):** one shared system serving all divisions — data/log + the measurement instruments + the portfolio platform (decide once; recommend extending the existing LMS). *Unblocks all baselines + every re-measure.*
- **② Growth-target increments** for each strand (baseline + the increment) — one sitting, after baselines land. *Unblocks all targets.*
- **③ Ownership + guardrails:** a lead owner for Technologically-fluent (the flagged gap); the values instrument; the bilingual-nudge tone guardrail; a pastoral-load ceiling for SAO.
- **④ Enablement:** protected PD time; ratify the single schoolwide communications + feedback plan.
- *Critical path: ① → ② → ③ → launch gates → start.*

## Two things this draft makes explicit
- **Stakeholders — parents especially — are a MEASURED dimension, not just an audience.** A third measurement track reads parent awareness/understanding/engagement with the mission/vision/SLOs (via Parents + the Parent Committee as a two-way partner), baselined at launch with mid-year and end-year targets, re-measured quarterly, seeding next year. (Precursor: parent feedback channels are currently unseeded and must be stood up.) Communication remains two-way and consolidated (one schoolwide channel), but parent *knowing* is now an outcome the plan measures.
- **Honest gaps:** the plan names where a strand is thinly delivered (Technologically-fluent skill; Physically-robust outside SAO) rather than papering over it — these are leadership decisions, surfaced.
